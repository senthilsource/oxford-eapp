4. The Apple Tree
There was an old apple tree in a farmer’s garden. It was so
old that it did not grow fruits anymore. But it was the home
of many birds, grasshoppers and squirrels.
One day, the farmer decided to cut the tree down because it
did not grow any fruits. He wanted to make some chairs from
the wood of the tree. When the farmer came to the tree
with his big axe, all the birds, grasshoppers and squirrels
who lived in it, made a lot of noise.
‘Please don’t cut this tree’ they begged the farmer. ‘It is our
only home’ they cried.
The farmer did not listen. Then the farmer saw a beehive on
the tree. It was full of honey. The farmer tasted the honey.
Yumm. It was very tasty!
The farmer realized that the tree was not useless after all!
He did not cut the tree. All the birds, grasshoppers and
squirrels were very happy.
5. The Elves and the Shoemaker
Once upon a time, there was a poor shoemaker. He worked
very hard. Still he could not earn enough money.
One day, he had enough leather to make only one pair of
shoes. He cut out the shoes, ready to stitch, and left them
on the table. Then he went to bed. He wanted to finish the
shoes on the next day and sell them.
Next morning, when he went to the table, what did he see!
The leather was gone. In its place was a beautiful pair of
shoes. But there was no one around. Who could have made
the shoes?
The shoemaker and his wife did not understand.
The shoe was sold and now the shoemaker had money to
buy more leather. That night he cut two shoes and went to
sleep. Again in the morning there were two perfect shoes
on his table. The shoemaker and his wife did not know how
this happened.
Again the shoemaker sold those shoes and made a lot of
money. He was very happy. That night he wanted to find out
who was making the beautiful shoes for him.
So he cut out four pairs of shoes and left them on his table.
He left a light in the room. Then, he and his wife hid behind
the curtains.
Late in the night, two tiny elves came dancing into the room.
They hopped on to the table and began to stitch the pieces
of the shoes. They stitched and they stitched. The elves
worked very hard. Soon the shoes were ready. The elves
then danced around the shoes and left the room q uickly.
The shoemaker and his wife wanted to thank the elves. So
the shoemaker’s wife made some very small pants, coats
and shoes. That night, they left the clothes on the table.
Then they hid behind the curtains to see.
The elves came at night and when they saw the small
clothes and shoes on the table, they were very happy. They
understood that these were gifts from the shoemaker. They
clapped and danced and wore their new clothes and went
away.
The elves did not come back again but the shoemaker now
had money to make many shoes. The shoemaker was not
poor anymore.
