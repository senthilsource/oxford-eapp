2. The Little Red Hen
The Little red hen lived on a big farm.
One morning, when she was looking for something to eat,
she found some grains of wheat.
‘Look!’ said the little red hen. ‘See what I have found. Who
will help me plant these grains of wheat?’
‘Not I,’ said the duck. ‘I am going to the pond to swim.’
‘Not I,’ said the cat. ‘I am going to sleep in the sun.’
‘Very well, I will plant the wheat then’, said the little red
hen. So she planted the wheat.
Soon the wheat began to grow. The little red hen asked,
‘Who will help me water the wheat?’
‘Not I,’ said the duck.
‘Not I’, s aid the cat.
‘Very well, I will water the wheat then’, said the little red
hen. So she watered the wheat.
After some time, the wheat became ripe. ‘It is time to cut
the wheat,’ said the little red hen. ‘Who will help me to cut
this wheat?’
‘Not I’, s aid the cat.
‘Not I’, s aid the duck.
‘Very well, then I will’, s aid the little red hen.
She cut the wheat very carefully.
Then the little red hen asked, ‘Now, who will take this wheat
to the mill to be made into flour?’
‘Not I’, s aid the duck.
‘Not I’, s aid the cat.
‘Very well, then’, said the little red hen, ‘I will take it myself ’.
So the little red hen went off to the mill. She came back with
a sack of flour.
‘Now, who is going to make this flour into bread?’ asked the
little red hen.
‘Not I’, s aid the duck.
‘Not I’, s aid the cat.
‘I will, then,’ said the little red hen. So she baked a very tasty
loaf of bread. It was beautiful and golden. The cat and the
duck looked at it hungrily.
‘Well now, who is going to eat this loaf of bread?’ asked the
little red hen.
‘I will’, s aid the duck quickly.
‘I will’, s aid the cat stepping close.
‘Oh, no, you won’t’, said the little red hen.
‘I am going to eat it myself ’.
The little red hen ate the whole loaf of bread.
Not a crumb was left for the lazy duck and cat.
3. Barry, the Bat
Barry the bat is just three years old.
Sometimes when Barry is sleeping upside down in his cave
he wakes up suddenly and is afraid of the light. Whenever
he becomes frightened like this, he calls his mother.
His mother comes flying over right away. ‘Are you all right
Barry’ she asks.
‘Well, I suddenly woke up, and I’m a little afraid of the light,’
Barry says.
‘Afraid of the light? You don’t need to be afraid of the light.
Here, let’s go to a darker part of the cave, so that you can
feel a little safer,’ says his mother.
‘But mom, can you leave the darkness on all day,’ says Barry.
‘If I need to get up to go to the bathroom, I don’t want it to
be all scary and light.’
‘Sure, Barry, I’ll leave the darkness on all day,’ says his
mother.
That way, if you need to go to the bathroom you can find
your way easily. ‘Thanks Mom. Please give me a hug before
going to sleep,’ says Barry.
